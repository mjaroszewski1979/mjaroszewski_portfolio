What is Microsoft | LinkedIn Learning and Who Is It For?

For those stumbling onto this review, let's break down what Microsoft and LinkedIn Learning are all about. LinkedIn Learning is a Massive Open Online Course (MOOC) platform owned by LinkedIn, the world's leading professional social network. Unlike other MOOC providers, LinkedIn Learning offers a unique educational experience tailored towards career-driven individuals. Subscribers, essentially regular individuals like you and me, gain unlimited access to a vast library of educational material crafted by specialists in their respective fields. The spectrum of topics covered is not only wide but also incredibly deep, ensuring that there's something for everyone seeking to advance their professional development.

The platform is distinguished by its exclusive focus on career-oriented content. This isn't the place to pick up macramé or salsa dancing skills; it's a dedicated space to fuel your professional growth. What's refreshing is that your subscription covers all educational endeavors without any hidden fees or premium courses that might catch you off guard. The content on LinkedIn Learning is structured into videos, courses, and learning paths. Completing a course or a learning path automatically earns you a certificate of completion, a valuable addition to your LinkedIn profile. Now, who would benefit from signing up?

  • People Who Want to Make Online Learning Part of Their Daily Lives: The platform offers a plethora of skill-oriented, easily digestible videos. With over 127,000 quick-to-watch videos in the "Creative" category alone, it caters to those who crave in-depth explanations and continuously seek smarter, faster ways of doing things in their jobs.
  • People Who Want to Advance Their Careers: Learning new skills not only enhances your job performance but also showcases your proactive approach to employers. LinkedIn Learning helps you acquire a set of new skills, proving to others that you're committed to adding more value in your position.
  • People Who Want to Make a Career Change: While it won't turn a copywriter into a civil engineer overnight, LinkedIn Learning provides resources for those looking to make a significant career change. Completing a learning path in a specific field, such as 3D modeling, can lay a foundation for transitioning into entry-level freelance gigs and eventually a new career.
  • People Looking to Maintain Their Professional Accreditation: Professionals like project managers, business analysts, accountants, and HR professionals can utilize LinkedIn Learning courses to acquire continuing education units (CEUs). The platform's partnerships with accrediting bodies ensure convenient access to relevant courses for maintaining professional certifications.
  • People Preparing for Certification Exams: LinkedIn Learning collaborates with major organizations for exam preparation content. While completing a course doesn't contribute to certification, it provides valuable insights into the knowledge required to ace certification exams. With 34 partnerships, including giants like Adobe and Amazon Web Services, the platform offers dedicated learning paths for various certification exams.

In a nutshell, LinkedIn Learning caters to a diverse audience, making it an invaluable resource for anyone seeking to integrate online learning into their daily lives, advance their careers, make a significant career change, maintain professional accreditation, or prepare for certification exams.


How Does LinkedIn Learning Work?

To unlock the rich educational offerings of LinkedIn Learning, the first step is to become a member of the LinkedIn social network. This requirement serves as a gateway to accessing the platform's expansive array of career-focused content. While some might see this as a hurdle, the reality is that anyone committed to advancing their career through online courses likely already has a presence on the world's largest professional social network. The heart of the LinkedIn Learning experience lies in its carefully curated content. The platform's user experience (UX) designers have excelled in creating a seamless interface for users to explore a diverse range of educational materials. LinkedIn Learning centers its focus on three primary fields: business, technology, and creative. Within each of these main categories are meticulously organized sub-categories, providing an exhaustive coverage of virtually every aspect of the main topics. The effort put into classifying content reflects a commitment to robust information architecture, ensuring users can easily navigate through the wealth of materials available.

Videos on LinkedIn Learning are bite-sized portions of educational content, each addressing a specific skill or concept. Although part of a larger learning experience within a course, these videos can be consumed as standalone lessons. This flexibility is advantageous for learners seeking specific information without committing to an entire course. Courses on LinkedIn Learning are cohesive sequences of videos, all designed, produced, and presented by the same specialist or group of educators. Unlike a mere collection of loosely related videos, each video within a course is meticulously crafted to fit seamlessly into the course's learning trajectory. Learning paths represent the next tier of content categorization on LinkedIn Learning. These are expertly curated selections of courses, aligning to help learners achieve specific, career-focused goals.

Upon completing a LinkedIn Learning course, learners earn a "certificate of completion" proudly displayed on their LinkedIn profile. The platform considers a course complete once all its video content has been watched. Some courses on LinkedIn Learning are affiliated with third-party accreditors like NASBA, PMI, HRCI, and IIBA. Completion of these courses, along with successful examination results, can earn learners continuous development credits from these accrediting bodies.


Choosing a LinkedIn Learning Course That’s Right for You

Before delving into LinkedIn Learning's vast library of over 16,000 courses, I harbored three specific concerns about finding the "right" course for me. These worries centered around the potential for duplicate material, the quality of courses, and the presence of outdated information. Considering the sheer volume of content, it seemed improbable for any platform to completely sidestep these challenges. And my skepticism was justified. Indeed, there are courses covering similar topics, variations in the informativeness of courses, and some dating back to 2014. However, despite these issues, LinkedIn Learning made it remarkably easy for me to discover a course tailored to my needs. Here's why:

  • No Limitations on Content Consumption: LinkedIn Learning operates on a subscription model that grants unlimited access to its content. If a particular course falls short of expectations, there's no financial penalty for trying out another one. It's a seamless process that ensures you can explore various courses until you find the perfect fit.
  • Get Acquainted with Your Instructors: LinkedIn Learning subjects all instructors to a rigorous vetting process. This commitment ensures that only qualified professionals contribute to the platform. To further boost confidence, learners can easily validate an instructor's credentials by perusing their LinkedIn profiles. This transparency adds a layer of assurance to the learning experience.
  • Continuous Updates and Additions: While some content may feel like it's been in the archives, LinkedIn Learning actively adds new courses and updates existing ones. Filtering search results by "Newest" proves invaluable in discovering the most current material. By staying open to trying new courses and filtering out potentially outdated content, I found it incredibly easy to identify the course that best suited my needs.

As mentioned earlier, LinkedIn Learning takes a serious approach to curating content and onboarding new instructors. However, learners can enhance their decision-making process by exploring the backgrounds of course instructors. This additional step ensures alignment with the instructor's expertise and teaching style, contributing to a more informed course selection. In conclusion, while challenges like duplicate content and varied course quality exist, the unique subscription model, instructor vetting process, and continuous updates make LinkedIn Learning a dynamic and user-friendly platform. With a bit of exploration and an openness to trying new courses, finding the ideal learning path becomes a straightforward and rewarding experience.


Course Sections and Overview

This comprehensive course is thoughtfully structured into six sections, each led by experts in their respective domains. The material is designed to be consumed in a specified order, but users have the flexibility to navigate through the content as per their preferences. Let's delve into each section with a reasonable level of detail:

  • 'Generative AI' Led by generative AI expert Pinar Seyhan Demirdag, this section provides a foundational understanding of generative AI. Topics covered include the basics of generative AI, its significance, differentiation from other AI types, working principles, content creation, types of models, future predictions, and ethical implications. Chapters include discussions on the importance of generative AI, tools for Generative AI, natural language models, Text to image applications, Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), VAE and Anomaly Detection, and the future of jobs in the age of Generative AI.
  • 'Generative AI: The Evolution of Thoughtful Online Search' Ashley Kennedy guides learners through the evolution of online search using generative AI. This section explores the distinctions and best practices of reasoning engines leveraging generative AI. Topics covered include the evolution of online information sharing, the comparison between search engines and reasoning engines, the future of online search, and harnessing the power of prompt engineering.
  • 'Streamlining Your Work with Copilot (formerly Bing Chat/Bing Chat Enterprise)' Jess Stratton takes learners through the practical application of Microsoft Bing Chat. The course demonstrates how Bing Chat can streamline various tasks, from generating ideas and summarizing data to solving common work problems. Learners gain insights into boosting productivity, conducting robust online searches, and automating work tasks.
  • 'Learning Microsoft 365 Copilot' Led by Nick Brazzi, this section introduces learners to Copilot and showcases its capabilities in generating drafts, creating presentations, and extracting key data trends. The course provides practical guidance on using Copilot across Microsoft 365 applications, including Word, Excel, Outlook, and Teams.
  • 'Ethics in the Age of Generative AI' Vilas Dhar addresses the ethical considerations surrounding generative AI, offering insights to designers and decision-makers. Chapters delve into distinguishing responsible tech from human behavior, understanding an ethical AI framework, applying the framework in real-world situations, and preparing organizations to address ethics in AI across stakeholders.
  • 'Introduction to Artificial Intelligence' Doug Rose broadens the scope, explaining that artificial intelligence involves more than just computer scientists. This section is designed for project managers, product managers, directors, executives, and students starting a career in AI. Key concepts covered include defining general intelligence, strong vs. weak AI, machine learning, artificial neural networks, searching for patterns in data, natural language processing, and an overview of tools in the field. This course provides a comprehensive journey into Generative AI and related technologies, catering to learners at various levels of expertise and roles within the industry. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced professional, each section offers valuable insights and practical knowledge.
